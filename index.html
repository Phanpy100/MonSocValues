MonSocValues

What is MonSocValues?

MonSocValues is a political quiz which tries to categorize the strains of Monarcho-Socialism in several boxes in questions, it's build on the 8values code.

What are the values?

There are six independent axes - Economic, Diplomatic, Constitutional, Cultural, Empowerment and Unity - and each has two opposing values assigned to them. They are:

Scientific (Economic)
Those with higher Scientific scores believe the economy should be distributed in a way similar to traditional leftist values, and at higher scores, Marxism

Co-operative (Economic)
Those with higher Market scores believe the economy should be regulated, but not fully embracing traditional leftist values, often third positionist, and at higher scores, Distributism.

Nation (Diplomatic)
Those with higher Nation scores are patriotic and nationalist. They often believe in an aggressive foreign policy, valuing the military, strength, sovereignty, and at high values, territorial expansion.

Internationalist (Diplomatic)
Those with higher Internationalist scores are cosmopolitan and globalist. They often believe in a peaceful foreign policy, emphasizing diplomacy, cooperation, integration, and at high values, a world government.

Constitution (Constitutional)
Those with higher Constitution scores believe in strong civil liberties. They tend to support parlimentary democracy and oppose absolutism, and at higher values anarcho-monarchism.

Absolutism (Constitutional)
Those with higher Authority scores believe in strong state power. Being directly by the king or a defacto dictator, and at high values even believing in divine right.

Reaction (Cultural)
Those with higher Reaction scores believe in traditional values and strict adherence to a moral code. Though not always, they are usually religious, and support the status quo or the status quo ante.

Progress (Cultural)
Those with higher Progress scores believe in social change and rationality. Though not always, they are usually secular or atheist, and support environmental action and scientific or technological research.

Revolt (Empowerment)
Those with higher Revolt scores believe that a democratic transition to Monarcho-Socialism is either impossible or unpreferable, and at higher scores even embracing values like permanent revolution.

Reform (Empowerment)
Those with higher Reform scores believe that a democratic transition to Monarcho-Socialism is preferable and possible, at higher scores embracing values similar to utopian socialism where we can simply convince the rich to join their cause instead.

Left Unity (Unity)
Those with higher Left Unity scores believe that you can better ally with socialists and other leftists rather then the monarchists.

Royal Unity (Unity)
Those with higher Royal Unity scores believe that the left only hold Monarcho-Socialism down as a joke and that Monarcho-Socialists should rather align themselves with existing monarchist movements.

What's the "Closest Match" mean at the bottom of the results?

In addition to matching you to the twelve values, the quiz also attempts to match you to a political ideology. This is a work in progress and is much less accurate than the values and axes, so don't take it too seriously. If you disagree with your assigned ideology, Please see below. Thanks!
